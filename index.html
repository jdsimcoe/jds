About Me

A Little Bit About Myself

I am first a Christian and husband, who desires and strives to glorify God in all aspects of life. I work as an experience designer with WebMD Health Services where I am helping push new standards like responsive design and adoption of HTML5 and CSS3 to produce better web experiences for our clients. Prior to WebMD, I worked at the Waggener Edstrom Studio D™ digital strategies practice, churning out creative Web, digital and print work for clients such as Microsoft, Bing, Verizon, HTC, Sasquatch! Music Festival, Washington Roundtable and the Jebsen Group.

Before Waggener Edstrom, I worked for four years in the financial industry, where I was responsible for driving the marketing and creative output of an investment consultancy. This role involved crafting the Web experiences, content, branding and graphic design for the agency, as well as distilling complicated investment concepts into forms that were easily digested by clients and investors.

I have worked on and off for the past six years doing freelance Web, graphic and print design work, consulting on the creative process with startups and developing a passion to think beyond visual design and dream up rich and usable experiences for consumers.

I have a passion for learning and am excited about the direction of the Web, as well as how technologies such as HTML5 and mobile-optimized design are facilitating great content and taking on a variety of forms to reach new audiences.

Design Philosophy

How I Think About Design

The beauty of the world is a communication of God's beauty.

—Jonathan Edwards

I believe that design is a powerful form of communication that mirrors the creative process that God weaved into the fabric of the universe we see and experience. Design is so much more than “aesthetics” or making things “look good”. Well-crafted design connects an audience with a message, intimating and evoking feeling, and inspiring action.

That ability to shape a user’s perception and experience drives me to create design that has meaning and purpose. Without purpose, design is merely a replicative process that can be copied or mimicked by anyone. True design inspires, communicates clearly and viscerally, and provides a powerful impetus to change the world around us.
